## The Problem

There are **13,000+ tutoring centers in Tunisia**. Almost none of them use digital tools beyond WhatsApp groups and Excel sheets.

Every week, the same four problems repeat across every single one of them:

### The Owner is flying blind
Riadh, 42, owns "Centre Excellence" in La Marsa. He has 35 students and 4 teachers. He finds out a student is failing when the *parent* calls him. He finds out a student left when the payment stops. Last month he lost 3 students — 540 TND in monthly recurring revenue — and had no warning, no reason, no data. He has never generated a single marketing post from a student's results, even though Ahmed went from 9/20 to 14/20 in 6 weeks.

### The Teacher walks in unprepared
Hatem, 31, teaches mathematics 5 days a week. Before every session, he has no idea which student struggled since last week, which topic caused it, or how to structure the next 90 minutes for maximum impact. He spends 10 minutes writing notes after every session. He has no idea if he's performing better or worse than last month. He feels undervalued and is considering moving to a competing center.

### The Student studies everything and nothing
Sana, 17, is failing algebra — but nobody told her the real reason: her fractions foundation is broken, and algebra depends on fractions. She studies algebra harder every week and gets worse. She has never received a personalized plan. She has never been told exactly what to fix and in what order. She attends less and less because it feels pointless.

### The Parent pays and hopes
Mme Mansouri pays 220 TND per month for her son Ahmed's sessions. She has no visibility into his progress between report cards — which come 3 times a year. She calls the center every two weeks to ask how he's doing. She has no idea her investment is working. When it does work, nobody captures that moment to generate a referral or a testimonial.

- Attendance slope (4x/week → 2x/week → 1x/week)
- Payment delay (on-time → 5 days late → 15 days late)
- Parent response time in messages (2 hours → 1 day → 3 days)
- Grade trend (even if still passing, a consistent downward slope)

Result: **"Karim: 78% chance of dropping next month."**

Not a feeling. Not a flag. A number the owner can act on before Karim makes a decision.
